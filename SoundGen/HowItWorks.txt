Sound Generation Functions
generate_beep() — a rising chirp

Linearly interpolates freq from 440 Hz → 880 Hz over 0.1 seconds
Computes a sine wave at each moment: sin(2π × freq × t)
Applies a square wave simplification: the sine only determines the sign (+32767 or -32767), making it a harsh buzzer rather than a smooth tone
Multiplies by a linear fade-out envelope (1 - t) so it dies away

generate_explosion() — a boom

Uses white noise: random.uniform(-1, 1) gives completely random samples, which sounds like static/hiss
Applies an exponential decay envelope e^(-5t) — loud at first, then drops off fast, mimicking an explosion fading

generate_crunch() — a short impact

Also white noise, but only 0.05 seconds long
Uses a quadratic decay (1-t)² which falls off sharper than linear, giving a punchy click
